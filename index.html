I'm Megan Barnett

A 4th year PhD Candidate in Geophysics at the University of Chicago.

About Me

I earned a BA in Astrophysics as well as Molecular and Cell Biology from the University of California, Berkeley in 2017 and came to the University of Chicago a year later after taking a gap year to continue to gain research experience. I am a first year graduate student here at the University of Chicago, and I'm excited to be pursuing a PhD in Geophysical Sciences.

Learn more

My Research Interests

My research interests lie in exploring the environmental conditions that make both solar system as well as extrasolar planets and moons potentially habitable. I am especially interested in the potential habitability and conditions of sub-surface global oceans, such as those on Europa and Enceladus, as well as the production/presence of organic molecules in planetary atmospheres and surfaces.
